History

The design of secure ‘digital cash’ systems was first postulated by David Chaum (1982) and the discourse continued (Chaum 1990; Tanaka, 1996; Szabo, 1997; Dai, 1998; Van Hove 2000) until 2008, when Satoshi Nakamoto introduced Bitcoin with a self-published paper (Nakamoto 2008) describing the blockchain ledger and inventing the first ‘cryptocurrency’. (Tarasiewicz, 2015)

Definition

focuses on the design
and characterisation of protocols that
govern the production, distribution, and
consumption of goods and services in a
decentralised digital economy.

Another Definition

the study of economic
interaction in an
adversarial environment.

Aims to create mechanisms
(rules) to produce an
equilibrium outcome.

Cryptoeconomics provides
a security guarantee for a
distributed system.

(e.g. 51% attacks are often economically unfeasable)
